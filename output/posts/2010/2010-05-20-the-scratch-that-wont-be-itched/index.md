I was in the aftermath of some seriously deep shit the first time M called out of the blue when I thought we were finally a continent apart. I remember walking aimlessly into some random North London park, settling on a bench and staring in fascination at some Orthodox Jewish boys - in their three-piece black suits and skull caps - playing badminton in the summer heat.

“Am in town, Wanna talk, drink or eat?” M tweeted.

I could never say no. Old habits and all.

We met up at the Britannia Gagosian where there was a JG Ballard tribute exhibit (not my idea) and moved to a pub in the area (my idea) to wash down the lurid aftertaste of some displayed surgical instruments and hi-res photos of crashed pilot body parts. 

That was when she started telling me that she had overstayed her residence visa and that she was on the run.

“Immigration storm trooper types were all over the underground last night. The trick here is to never bat an eyelash, be as deadpan as can be. Earphones are always handy so don’t run or act suspicious if they do the stop and search thing.... “

“What are you talking about? I thought you had the right to remain after that scholarship grant?”  But she wasn’t even listening.

“What I know is that they have no right to ask for documentation or biometric crap. Just basic ID stuff. So I hope you don’t mind that I gave them your name, post code, date of birth.    

“You what? ??!"

We were the same age, same racial profile. And now the same side of the binary tick-box. How convenient. My old flame and I have become one, finally. Never imagined it this way though.  

She was lucky to do that during an era of flawed face recognition and biometric fingerprint software which she was fully geared for then, before Home Office went full on with the DNA database crap. 

“They did take note but they let me go. So if they get back to you – you know where you were….Anyway, we all look alike to them.” She said just like that, like it was a minor inconvenience.

\*\*\*

“Am in town, Wanna talk, drink or eat?” Our second meal here in the EU was a decade later over curry wurst and shared swigs of Bruichladdich, beside that Kaiser Wilhelm Bombed-out church in Berlin.

M had just told me that her current partner was spear-fishing somewhere in what used to be Palawan and getting the full rock star treatment from the locals. Things never change, really. 

 “Oh Fortress Europe,” she started addressing the structure, oblivious to the stares of tourists passing by.

“Let’s all raise a toast to this maximum surveillance museum we have learned to call home.” The street food was cheap but the whiskey she brought along was Nikka, 51.4% strong.

“Fortress…”  I found myself muttering. “Your citizens are well-preserved and still bored enough to seek some orientalist adventure in the place we grew up.”

Then she brought out some leftover space cakes which were back in fashion as were the coffeehouse revivals. They were lousy and I refused to take more than the first bite which simultaneously managed to loosen my tongue and leave that swimming in syrup sensation of sports event reruns in slo-mo each time I had to turn my head.

“Blast from the past! Did I ever tell you what happened after you left London? Did you ever wonder if they knocked on my door after stopping you on the tube?” This church can survive more bombs.

She gave me a puzzled look like it was a vague memory to her.

“They never asked me where I was, but other kinds of authorities did. It happened on the same evening of the lowest point in my life and you gave me the best alibi ever. The records maintain that I was stopped on the tube and this happened at the same time that I almost killed some knife-wielding dickhead, trying to defend myself.”

“Wow.” she said while flicking off crumbs from her layers of clothes.  

Maybe I wasn’t that coherent.  Maybe she wasn’t even capable of listening in that state.  Fine with me. At least that was out, though many things were still left unsaid between us.

\*\*\*

“Am in town, Wanna talk, drink or eat?”  Meeting number three, I forget the year, found us both homesick for the tropics as we lingered in the Southeast Asian section of the Aarhus Botanical Gardens with its thermostat jacked up in the middle of the Danish winter. Memories of ex-homelands triggered by the smell of Philippine soil and the scent of _dalandans_ turning ripe in the precisely configured humidity.   

Then she told me she volunteered to go through the controversial Longevity™ treatments, before everyone, including holdouts, (like me) decided it wasn’t a bad idea.

“Remember going to the orthodontist for tweakings? That’s all there is to it. Many visits to a clinic won’t actually make you feel a major transformation is taking place until you’re done. Oh and the pill diet, of course.” 

M was always the early adopter.

“But why, really? What’s the point?  Why choose to live long in this kind of world?” I remember asking her after a beat.

“I am scared to die without having changed the world.” M replied.

“How utterly…. Romantic, whatever that means.”

And that was the last thing I said to her, at that last time we both agreed to meet. 

It’s been years, and eventually I found myself riding what became the bandwagon of Longevity™. I guess I was just scared to die cynical and numb. I had no longer had her grand dreams to change a world that had already changed me. And yet.  
  
Why am I still holding my breath?
