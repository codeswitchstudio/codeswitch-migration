Logo and branding project request from the new Saw Swee Hock Southeast Asia Centre launching in January 2015.  <!--more-->Initial photo peg was the HK skyline which was replaced with the KL skyline.

![seac_logo_peg](images/seac_logo_peg-274x300.png)

![seac_logo_draft1](images/seac_logo_draft1-300x224.png)

 

 

The image of the skyline was too busy so I focused on the Southeast Asian boat, which was good and ripe with symbolism.

[![seac_logo_draft2](images/seac_logo_draft2-300x224.png)![seac_logo_draft3](images/seac_logo_draft3-300x230.png)](http://www.codeswitchstudio.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/SEAC_yellow_Twitter.png)

[![seac_logos_yellow](images/seac_logos_yellow-189x300.png)](http://www.codeswitchstudio.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/seac_logos_yellow.png) ![seac_logo_brand_yellow_2](images/seac_logo_brand_yellow_2-300x202.png)![seac_logo_brand_yellow](images/seac_logo_brand_yellow-300x273.png)

The SSH-SEAC director loved the boat with the triangles. Yay!

Next day he changed his mind.  There an issue with colours  (yellow conveyed different things in different Southeast Asian countries, changing it to red didn't help either.) So it was back to the drawing board.

To cut a long story short, this eventually became the approved logo.  Since the colours were ditched, it would be pointless to use the modular triangles to form the S.  In the end, we were able to keep the boat and the letter S. Not all was lost.
