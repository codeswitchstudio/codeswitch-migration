This pandemic has seen me trying to regain a foothold on Web Dev, Modern Javascript, and Python.1 Hence am reactivating myself in other spaces. (Let's see if I can actually get anything done.)

- [Substack](https://indicodes.substack.com/) - dispatches on my so-called coding journey plus a bunch of distractions that get me going along the way
- [T](https://twitter.com/iendaya_LDN)[witter](https://twitter.com/IE62611037) \- I only ever use this to retweet funny stuff or for promos, make a customer query. I know I shouldn't make a new account but some people from a former life actually follow me and would be weirded out by code-oriented tweets.
- [Github](https://github.com/codeswitchstudio) - I opened this a while back only to look at some design tools but I hope to be using the repositories soon
- [CodePen](https://codepen.io/icetanc) - I opened this previously when I was doing Freecodecamp exercises. I was able to go through HTML and CSS ones but got stuck on the certification tasks. I suppose I can go back to it.

Am pretty sure I want to do something in the direction of creative coding while trying to master Javascript and do Front-end stuff. I also want to naturally ease back into responsive design and UX-ish things and acquire more information design skills.

So far I want to:

- complete exercises from [The Coding Train](https://thecodingtrain.com/Courses/data-and-apis/)
- be productive on [GitHub](https://thecodingtrain.com/beginners/git-and-github/)
- go with Aldo to the next MozFest
- be qualified enough to [volunteer](https://www.womenwhocode.com/london) and be part of a team doing good things
- be able to immerse in coding a couple of good creative projects that tell stories visually (am inclined towards a cross between the CD-ROM content of the late 90s and some low-fi Indie Games with zine aesthetics and values that appeal to me) I know [there's hope for me yet](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UFYJ2DE9wlM&list=WL&index=7&ab_channel=GreatBigStory).
- build a responsive and dynamic landing page from scratch without using Wordpress anymore
- keep on blogging over at substack.
